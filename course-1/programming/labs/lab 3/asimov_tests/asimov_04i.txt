Don't worry, be happy!

The date was August 1, 1941. World War II had been raging for two years. France
had  fallen,  the  Battle  of Britain had been fought, and the Soviet Union had
just  been invaded by Nazi Germany. The bombing of Pearl Harbor was four months 
in the future.

But  on  that  day,  with Europe in flames, and the evil shadow of Adolf Hitler
apparently  falling  over  all  the  world,  what  was chiefly on my mind was a 
meeting toward which I was hastening.
